Rotten potatoes - Documentation

This web application will provide access to information related to movies, movie genres, directors and other movie-related content. A user can sign up, add personal information and create and store a list of movies they deem to be their favourite. The application interacts with a database which stores movie-related data. Below you can find further details on each available endpoint, includng logic, method, a short description, the request format and the response format!

Endpoints:

Logic Method Short Description Request format Response format
'/movies' GET Contains data on all available movies. None Contains all available movies.
/movies/:title' GET Contains data on a single movie using the movie title. None Contains data such as a short description, director, genre and an image URL.
'/movie/:release' GET Contains data on movies by the release year. None Contains data on movies by release year.
'/genre/:name' GET Contains data on movies by genre. None Contains data on movies by genre.
'/directors/:name' GET Contains data about a director. None Contains data about a director.
'/users' POST User registration. Contains data that a new user inputs.

(username: 'Jimmy Jo', password:'jimmyjospw', email:'jimmy@jo.com', dateofbirth:'01.02.1987')

 Contains a text notification that the user has been successfuly added.
'/users/:username/:password/:email/:dateofbirth' PUT Update user information The user can update their username, password, email or date of birth. Text notification that the user information was updated.
'/favorites/:username/:title' POST Adding a movie to the user´s favourite list. None Text notification that the movie has been added to the favourite list.
'/favorites/:username/:title' DELETE Removing a movie from the user´s favourite list. None Text notification that the movie has been removed from the favourite list.
'/users/:username' DELETE User deregistration None Text notification that the user has been deregistered.

Genre Table:

*Descriptions taken from Wikipedia
GenreID Name Description
1 Action An action story is similar to adventure, and the protagonist usually takes a risky turn, which leads to desperate situations (including explosions, fight scenes, daring escapes, etc.). Action and adventure are usually categorized together (sometimes even as "action-adventure") because they have much in common, and many stories fall under both genres simultaneously (for instance, the James Bond series can be classified as both).
2 Adventure An adventure story is about a protagonist who journeys to epic or distant places to accomplish something. It can have many other genre elements included within it, because it is a very open genre. The protagonist has a mission and faces obstacles to get to their destination. Also, adventure stories usually include unknown settings and characters with prized properties or features.
3 Comedy Comedy is a story that tells about a series of funny, or comical events, intended to make the audience laugh. It is a very open genre, and thus crosses over with many other genres on a frequent basis.
4 Crime A crime story is about a crime that is being committed or was committed. It can also be an account of a criminal's life. It often falls into the action or adventure genres.
5 Drama Within film, television and radio (but not theatre), drama is a genre of narrative fiction (or semi-fiction) intended to be more serious than humorous in tone,[2] focusing on in-depth development of realistic characters who must deal with realistic emotional struggles. A drama is commonly considered the opposite of a comedy, but may also be considered separate from other works of some broad genre, such as a fantasy.
6 Fantasy A fantasy story is about magic or supernatural forces, rather than technology (as science fiction) if it happens to take place in a modern or future era. Depending on the extent of these other elements, the story may or may not be considered to be a "hybrid genre" series; for instance, even though the Harry Potter series canon includes the requirement of a particular gene to be a wizard, it is referred to only as a fantasy series.
7 Animation Although animation is listed under "Genres" and is classified as a genre by many film critics and streaming services, there is an ongoing debate between the animation community and the general public whether animation is a genre or a medium; and that the genres in the "Live-action scripted" genre can also be portrayed in an animated format, and the below kinds of animation are not types of stories, but simply types of ways that a film can be animated.
8 Mystery A mystery story follows an investigator as they attempt to solve a puzzle (often a crime). The details and clues are presented as the story continues and the protagonist discovers them and by the end of the story the mystery/puzzle is solved. For example, in the case of a crime mystery the perpetrator and motive behind the crime are revealed and the perpetrator is brought to justice. Mystery novels are often written in series, which facilitates a more in-depth development of the primary investigator. Specific types of mystery story include locked room mysteries and cozy mysteries.
9 Science fiction(Sci-Fi) Science fiction is similar to fantasy, except stories in this genre use scientific understanding to explain the universe that it takes place in. It generally includes or is centered on the presumed effects or ramifications of computers or machines; travel through space, time or alternate universes; alien life-forms; genetic engineering; or other such things. The science or technology used may or may not be very thoroughly elaborated on; stories whose scientific elements are reasonably detailed, well-researched and considered to be relatively plausible given current knowledge and technology are often referred to as hard science fiction.
10 Thriller A Thriller is a story that is usually a mix of fear and excitement. It has traits from the suspense genre and often from the action, adventure or mystery genres, but the level of terror makes it borderline horror fiction at times as well. It generally has a dark or serious theme, which also makes it similar to drama.
